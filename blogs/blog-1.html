Importance and Urgency in the life of a programmer

Author : Isaac Ssemugenyi

In everyone's day to day life, we are presented with different challenges of life, however small or big the challenges of life may be, this makes us take different actions based on our own life setup. We at times find that we can treasure what we consider more important in our life, we can also consider what gives us the immediate pleasure or what gives us a quick success in life, also at time we prioritize what we can finish the fastest. However, these decisions that we make on a daily basis end up defining our whole life and we end up living by them. These in the end impact us as people either positively or negatively making us more successful and fulfilled or less successful and not fulfilled.

This brings us to the Time Matrix, which defines how one specifically a programmer decides on how to spend their time, remember time waits for no one, the way one decides to spend their time becomes a key fact in how one's life will turn out to be in a lifetime.

The Time Matrix talks about the 4 quadrants, where the first quadrant (Q1) specifies the "Important and Urgent" challenges and the second quadrant (Q2) specifies the "Important but Not Urgent" challenges, the third quadrant (Q1) specifies the "Not Important but Urgent" challenges and lastly the fourth quadrant (Q1) specifying "Not Important and Not Urgent" challenges. The four quadrants represent what we do on a daily basis and it entirely reflects how we make day to day decisions on doing this.

By choosing a few examples to reflect on, in the first quadrant is where we like to do tasks at the rush hours when the deadlines are yet to be due, attend to urgent meetings and before we realize eventually we are totally worn out and can no longer be productive and effective, in the second quadrant is where we have a task that is important but not so urgent and has human beings and developers tend to ignore this quadrant for good yet it is within this quadrant that people develop a habit of daily practice that makes them more effective and move people mountains. In quadrant three lies our distractions, we consider these urgent but forget that they are not important at all, these carry along with the irrelevant meetings (unplanned) that take more time than they would take, attending to people's minor issues and unimportant emails, phone calls, commenting on posts on Facebook excessively and watching other people's status in times you would be doing something productive, in quadrant three and four we consume more time than we can possibly think as they are more relaxing and addictive in nature.

Fight the temptation of submitting to Q1 and Q3, do away with Q4 and concentrate on Q2, this is the source of success and role modeling. The quadrant of fulfillment and achievement of the personal mission statement.
